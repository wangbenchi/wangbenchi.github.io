AM-lab achieves great success at APCV2024

From July 10th to 12th, 2024, Professor Wang Benchi and Professor Huang Zhehao led a delegation from the AM-lab research group to participate in the 16th Asia Pacific Conference on Vision (APCV2024) at the National University of Singapore. During this international event, Professor Huang Zhehao and 7 members of the research group delivered talks on the neural mechanisms of working memory, attention and eye movements, visual search and attentional capture, highlighting the group's research achievements and progress.

Throughout the conference, the research group actively participated in various academic activities, sharing research experiences and findings with other teams from all over the world. They discussed research methodologies, exchanged ideas, and explored potential future collaboration opportunities. These engagements not only broadened the academic horizons of the research group members but also infused new vigor into the group's future academic development.

After the conclusion of APCV2024, AM-lab members gather for a group photo to celebrate the successful academic communication.

The participation in APCV2024 provided valuable learning and growth opportunities for all members and significantly bolstered the research group's presence in the international academic community. The AM-lab research group remains dedicated to rigorous scientific inquiry and looks forward to achieving further advancements in the field of visual science in their ongoing academic endeavors.
